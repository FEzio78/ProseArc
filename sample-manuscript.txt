The Shadow Blade

The rain had not stopped for three days. Kael stood at the edge of the cliff, watching the grey sea churn beneath him, and wondered whether the storm was a warning or simply the world being indifferent as it always was.

He had carried the Shadow Blade for a year now. It was lighter than any sword had a right to be, and colder, as if it remembered every winter it had ever passed through. The old woman in the market had called it cursed. Kael preferred to think of it as honest.
